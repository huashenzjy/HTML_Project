## Why This Method?

You have one main problem right now.
You make the **same grammar mistakes in every essay**.
This will not fix itself by writing more essays.
You need a loop:

> Write → Check yourself → Compare with correct version → Record your mistakes → Avoid them next time

This is called a **feedback loop**. It is the fastest way to fix grammar errors.

## Daily Routines in Detail

---

### Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday — Translation Practice (25 minutes)

#### Step 1 — Review Yesterday's Mistakes (5 minutes)
- Open Obsidian
- Read the 2–3 mistake sentences you recorded yesterday
- Just read them. Do not do any exercises.
- This helps your brain remember what to avoid today.

#### Step 2 — Translation Practice — THE MOST IMPORTANT STEP (10 minutes)
- Open today's VOA story in Chinese
- **Do not look at the English original**
- Type the English translation yourself (about 8–10 sentences)
- While you write, ask yourself one question only:
  **"Is this verb form correct?"**
- Do not stop to look up words. Just finish writing first.

#### Step 3 — Check Yourself (5 minutes)
Do not send to AI yet. First, check your own work:
- [ ] Is every verb form correct? (base form / past simple / past participle)
- [ ] Did I use "had + verb" too much? Does each one really need past perfect?
- [ ] Are my relative clauses complete? (which / that / who + subject + verb)

#### Step 4 — Compare with AI + Record Mistakes (5 minutes)
Send your translation to ChatGPT. Use this exact prompt every time:

```
I am a B1 English learner.
Correct my grammar errors only.
Focus on: verb form, verb tense (especially past perfect misuse), and clause structure.
Explain each correction briefly.
Do not change my vocabulary or ideas.
```

Then:
1. Compare the AI version with the original VOA article
2. Write your mistakes in your Obsidian error table
3. If you made the same mistake as yesterday, write "again" next to it

---

#### Your Minimum Rule (Never Break This)
> On busy days or lazy days: translate only 5 sentences. That's it. 5 minutes.
> This rule cannot be broken.
> Why: Starting is the hardest part. Once you start, you will usually finish.

---

### Wednesday — Grammar Focus Day (25 minutes)

#### Step 1 — Find This Week's Most Common Mistake (5 minutes)
- Open Obsidian
- Look at this week's mistake records
- Which type of mistake appeared the most?

#### Step 2 — Read the Grammar Rule (10 minutes)
- Find that mistake's unit in *English Grammar in Use*
- Read it once
- Close the book

#### Step 3 — Write 8 Sentences (10 minutes)
- Write 8 sentences about your own life using that grammar rule
- Send to ChatGPT for correction
- Record any mistakes in Obsidian

---

### Saturday — Writing Day (30 minutes)

#### Step 1 — Write a Short Essay, 150–200 words (20 minutes)

Choose one topic:
- "Describe a habit you changed and why."
- "What is the hardest thing about living in New York?"
- "Describe a person who influenced you."
- "What is one goal you are working on right now?"
- Or use a topic from your KBCC class.

**Required rules (your essay is not finished without these):**
- You must use at least 1 relative clause (which / that / who)
- You must use at least 1 adverbial clause (because / although / when)
- In story paragraphs, do not use past perfect unless you are sure it is needed

#### Step 2 — Check Yourself + AI Correction (10 minutes)
- Check yourself first (same checklist as translation days)
- Then send to ChatGPT with the same prompt
- Record all corrections in Obsidian

---

### Sunday — Review + Prepare Next Week (20 minutes)

#### Step 1 — Weekly Review (10 minutes)
Look at all your Obsidian records from this week. Answer these questions:
1. What was my most common mistake this week?
2. Did any mistake type appear less than last week?
3. What is the one thing I need to be most careful about next week?

#### Step 2 — Prepare Next Week's Materials (10 minutes)
1. Go to `learningenglish.voanews.com`
2. Find 5 new Level 2 or Level 3 stories
3. Copy each story into ChatGPT and ask for a Chinese translation
4. Save each one in Obsidian with these names:
   - Monday Material
   - Tuesday Material
   - Wednesday Material
   - Thursday Material
   - Friday Material

> After this step, next week you can open Obsidian and start immediately.
> No time wasted searching for materials.

## How to Stop Being Lazy: 8 Rules

### Rule 1 — Attach to an Existing Habit
Connect English practice to something you already do every day.
Example: Right after dinner, open Obsidian.
Do not rely on willpower. Rely on a trigger.
**Today, decide which habit you will attach it to.**

### Rule 2 — Keep the Minimum Rule
On lazy days: 5 sentences. That is all. 5 minutes.
This rule cannot be broken.
Once you start, you will almost always continue.

### Rule 3 — Track Your Streak
Write "Day ___" at the top of every log.
Breaking a 20-day streak feels painful. Use that feeling.
The minimum rule (5 sentences) still counts as a complete day.

### Rule 4 — Make Progress Visible
Every week, record your most common mistake in Obsidian.
After one month, look back at Week 1. You will see the mistakes are fewer.
This visible progress is more motivating than any goal.

### Rule 5 — Be Specific About When and Where
Fill in the promise at the top of this plan right now.
Do not write "every evening."
Write "every day at 9:30 PM, sitting at my desk."
Research shows that being specific about time and place increases success by 3 times.

### Rule 6 — Plan Your Rewards Now
Decide now, before you start:
- 30 days in a row → reward: _______________
- 60 days in a row → reward: _______________
Write this in the promise section. Do not wait until later.

### Rule 7 — Build Your Identity
Every Sunday in your review, write this one sentence:
**"I am a person who practices English every day."**
This sounds simple, but it works. People who connect habits to identity are much more likely to continue long-term.

### Rule 8 — Never Miss Two Days in a Row
You will miss a day. That is normal.
The only rule: **never miss two days in a row.**
Missing one day is an accident.
Missing two days starts to become a new habit — the habit of not practicing.
The day after you miss, do the minimum rule (5 sentences), no matter what.
